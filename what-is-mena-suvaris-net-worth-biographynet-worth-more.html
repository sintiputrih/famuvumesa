Suvari made the chunk of her wealth from paychecks from movie roles.

Who is Mena Suvari?

Mena Alexandra Suvari is the daughter of Candice Chambers and Ando Ivar Suvari who was born on February 13, 1979 in Newport, Rhode Island in the United States of America. Her mother was a nurse while her father was a psychiatrist.

Suvari has six other siblings; five brothers, Agu, Madis, Ando Junior, Sulev and Yuri (Jüri) and sister Mari-Ann. Mena Suvari attended Rocky Hill Country Day School before going to Providence High School, Burbank to further her education.

Suvari began a career in modeling with Millie Lewis Models and Talent as a preteen. She quickly gained recognition and appeared in a Rice-A-Roni commercial. At age 12, she was raped by her then 16 year old boyfriend.

Suvari initially had the dream of becoming an archaeologist, astronaut, or doctor when a modeling agency stopped by her all-girls school, Ashley Hall, to offer classes. At the time of her emergence in the acting field, she has already made big strides in the modeling world as she had modelled for the New York based Wilhelmina agency for five years.

Suvari became a household name after her appearances in the critically acclaimed comedy-drama “American Beauty”, which earned her a BAFTA nomination for Best Supporting Actress. She made other notable appearances in the latter part of 1999 and early 2000s including “American Pie,” “Kiss the Girls,” “Slums of Beverly Hills,” “The Rage: Carrie 2,” “Sugar & Spice, The Musketeer” among several others.

Mena Suvari has been married three times and have a child. Her first marriage was with German-born cinematographer Robert Brinkman on March 2000 which ended in a divorce in 2005. She married for a second time and this time it was to Simone Sestito from 2010 to 2012 before finally tying the knot with Michael Hope in 2018. As of now, she is still married to him.

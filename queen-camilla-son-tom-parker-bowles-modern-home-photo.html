Queen Camilla's son Tom Parker Bowles' home is so unexpected

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Saturday, October 5, 2024

The Former MasterChef star lives in west London and you won't believe his home

    MasterChef star Tom Parker Bowles is the son of the Queen Camilla, and his London home is nothing like his mother's traditional residences, Clarence House, Highgrove House and Buckingham Palace. 

    The cookbook writer gave a very rare glimpse inside his home when he appeared on Loose Women via video call. While speaking to host Charlene White, Tom sat on a grey velour chair inside his open-plan house - and it's so stylish!

    Behind him, fans could see his contemporary kitchen with white cupboards and a stainless-steel extractor fan. Next to the cooking area, Tom has a workspace which has a Mac computer and is piled high with lots of books – no doubt some copies of his seven cookbooks are on the shelves.

    In the background, Tom's patio doors can be seen which presumably open onto a garden space, and there is also an interesting spiral staircase leading to the first floor of the building. The modern staircase also has a stylish light pendant hanging from it, bringing a touch of glamour to the space.

    Tom's modern home is worlds away from his mother's regal homes – in particular, her official London residence with Prince Charles, Clarence House, is filled with ornate furnishes and grand elements. The son of the Duchess grew up at Bolehyde Manor and Middlewick House in Wiltshire, and both are very stunning country homes.

    The food writer split from his wife Sarah Buys in 2018 and then began dating former journalist Alice Procope for almost two years before she sadly died of cancer at the age of 42. Tom shares two children with Sarah, Lola, 13, and Freddie, 11, and he was busy helping with homeschooling during the pandemic.

    Tom, whose father Andrew Parker Bowles is Camilla's first husband, is very close with King Charles and previously said he has "always adored" his stepdad.

    He told in 2015: "All you care about your parents is they're happy and my mother is exceptionally happy at the moment. I've always adored my stepfather; he's always been a kind and good and lovely man.

Overview of Roy Scheider’s Life and Career

Roy Scheider was born on November 10, 1932, in Orange, New Jersey, United States. He began his acting career in the 1960s and quickly rose to prominence with his role in “The French Connection” in 1971. However, it was his role as Chief Martin Brody in Steven Spielberg’s “Jaws” that catapulted him to international fame. Throughout his career, Scheider was nominated for two Academy Awards and won a Golden Globe Award.

Where was Nathan Fielder? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

Nathan Joseph Fielder was born on 12th May 1983 and is currently 38 years old. He was born in Vancouver, British Columbia, and holds Canadian citizenship. Likewise, he is a Christian and comes from a Jewish family. His solar sign is Taurus, as he was born on May 12th.

His parents, Deb Fielder, and Eric Fielder, gave birth to him. Both his mother and father were social workers. Together with his parents, he was raised in Vancouver.

He received his education at Point Grey Secondary School, according to his background. He was a part of the school’s comedy group throughout his tenure there. Seth Rogen, the famous comedian, was also a member. He worked as a magician during his adolescence. Also, he belongs to the California-based comedy club known as The Magic Hustle.

Following high school, he enrolled at the University of Victoria to study business. He received a Bachelor of Business degree in 2005. After finishing, he relocated to Toronto and enrolled in a Humber College comedy program. Before leaving to pursue a career in comedy, he briefly worked for a brokerage company.

How did Nathan Fielder start his Professional Career?

Career In 2006, Nathan started working as a writer for the singing competition program Canadian Idol. Michael Donovan, the executive producer of the CBC comedy series, noticed him while he was there working. He was then employed by Donovan as a field reporter.

He wrote and produced a large number of skits for Demetri Martin’s “Important Things” second season in 2010. As a guest voice actor, he also appeared in the “Bob’s Burgers” series finale.

He played a boom operator in the television series “Jon Benjamin Has a Van” in 2011. He also played Bob Woodward in a “Drunk History” episode. Similarly, two of his other movies include “The Night Before” (2015) and “The Disaster Artist” (2017).

Then, he became well-known after debuting his program, “Nathan for You.” He wrote, directed, and starred in the show. In 2019, he signed a contract with HBO, and he started producing the documentary series “How To with John Wilson” as executive producer.

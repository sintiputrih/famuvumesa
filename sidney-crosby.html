Sidney Crosby is a Canadian ice hockey player for the National Hockey League captain. Sidney Crosby has even led the Pittsburgh Penguins to three Stanley Cup championships (2009, 2016, and 2017).

Early Life and Childhood

Sidney Crosby was born on August 7, 1987, in Halifax, Nova Scotia, to his father Troy Crosby, and mother Trina Crosby. He is currently 34 years old. Sidney and his younger sister Taylor are two children to his parents. He grew up in Cole Harbour. His father was a goaltender for the Verdun Junior Canadiens.

Education

From age 12 to 15, Crosby attended Astral Drive Junior High School and transferred to Shattuck-Saint Mary’s in Faribault, Minnesota, to play with the school’s hockey program.  Besides, even played for the Rimouski Océanic of the Quebec Major Junior Hockey League. Crosby attended and graduated in 2005 from Harrison Trimble High School, in Moncton, New Brunswick.

Professional Life

Sidney Crosby’s father used to take Sidney with him to hockey. Crosby used to hockey on his own in the basement at the age of two and by the age of 3, he already knew how to ice skate. At the age of 10, he made himself significantly different, from other kids of his age through his talent. For the local team match, he scored 159 in 55 games. Extraordinary!

By the time he graduated high school, he was a sensation in the locality. He got the chance to work with retiring Penguins superstar Mario Lemieux. Crosby quickly acclimated to the NHL, taking the ice as the team’s best player. Crosby had emerged as one of the league’s best young players. In 2009, he got to hold the Stanley Cup, hockey’s ultimate prize.

Despite his injury missing 29 games in 2007-08, he finally, come back the next year leading his team to the finals of the Stanley Cup, in which they were defeated by the Red Wings. In 2008, he scored 100 goals, 200 assists, and 300 points and registered his name National Hockey League to be the youngest player to that extent.

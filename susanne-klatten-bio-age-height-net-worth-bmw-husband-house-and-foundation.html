Susanne Klatten Biography

As of May 2021, Susanne’s net worth was estimated at US$25.5 billion, ranking her the richest woman in Germany as well as the 65th richest person in the world according to the Bloomberg Billionaires Index.

Susanne Klatten Education

After gaining a degree in business finance, Susanne worked for the advertising agency Young & Rubicam in Frankfurt from the year 1981 to 1983. This was followed by a course in marketing as well as management at the University of Buckingham, as well as an MBA from IMD Business School in Lausanne specializing in advertising.

Susanne gained further business experience in London with Dresdner Bank, the Munich branch of management consultants McKinsey, and the bank Bankhaus Reuschel & Co. Susanne has often worked under the name Susanne Kant.

Susanne Klatten BMW

On her father’s death, Susanne inherited his 50.1% stake in pharmaceutical and chemicals manufacturer Altana, Susanne sits on Altana’s supervisory board as well as helped transform it into a world-class corporation in the German DAX list of 30 top companies.

In the year 2006 Altana AG sold its pharmaceutical activities to Nycomed for €4.5 billion, leaving only its specialty chemicals business, The €4.5 billion was distributed to shareholders as a dividend.

Altana maintained its stock exchange listing, as well as Klatten, remained its majority shareholder. In 2009, Susanne bought almost all shares she did not already own in Altana. ALTANA and SKion, which are both wholly owned by Susanne, are shareholders of Landa Digital Printing with together 46 percent since 2018.

Landa Digital Printing is a company of Israeli entrepreneur as well as inventor Benny Landa in the field of digital printing and nanotechnology.

Susanne’s father also left her a 12.50% stake in BMW, but following the death of her mother in the year 2015, her stake in BMW is now 19.2%. Susanne was appointed to the supervisory board of BMW with her brother Stefan Quandt in the year 1997.

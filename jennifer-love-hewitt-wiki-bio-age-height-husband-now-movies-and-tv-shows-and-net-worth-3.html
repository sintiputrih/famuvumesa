Jennifer Love Hewitt Biography and Wiki

Jennifer Love Hewitt is an American actress, singer, songwriter, producer and director. She started her career as a child actress and singer. Jennifer appeared in national television commercials before joining the cast of the Disney Channel series Kids Incorporated (1989–1991). She also performed as a backup singer before recording her debut studio album at the age of 12, Love Songs (1992), which was released exclusively in Japan.

Jennifer received her breakthrough role as Sarah Reeves Merrin on the Fox teen drama Party of Five (1995–1999). She rose to fame as a teen star for her role as Julie James in the horror film I Know What You Did Last Summer (1997) and its 1998 sequel.

Jennifer starred as Melinda Gordon on the CBS supernatural drama Ghost Whisperer from 2005 to 2010 , for which she received two Saturn Awards in 2007 and 2008.  Later, she starred on the Lifetime drama series The Client List from 2012 to 2013, and was previously nominated for a Golden Globe Award for the pilot film.

She starred as Special Agent Kate Callahan on the CBS crime drama Criminal Minds from 2014 to 2015.  Apart from acting, she has served as a producer on some of her film and television projects. She was identified as the “number one reader choice” on the November 1999 and May 2009 covers of Maxim magazine.  She was  named the sexiest woman on television in 2008 by TV Guide.

She starred in the Fox first responder drama series 9-1-1, playing 9-1-1 dispatcher Maddie Buckley beginning in 2018.

It is the longest-running primetime series in the history of television, it first aired in 1951 and continues into the present day. Since 1954 all of its productions have been broadcast in color. The film  is one of the first video productions to telecast in color,  a rarity in the 1950s.

Jennifer Love Hewitt Scary Movie

Scary Movie is a  horror comedy film directed by Keenen Ivory Wayans. It  is a parody of the horror, slasher, and mystery film genres. Several mid- and late-’90s films and TV shows are spoofed, and the script is primarily based on the ’90s hit horror films Scream (1996) and I Know What You Did Last Summer (1997).

The first in the Scary Movie film series, it was followed by four sequels: Scary Movie 2 (2001), Scary Movie 3 (2003), Scary Movie 4 (2006), and Scary Movie 5 (2013).

In spite of a mixed critical reception, the film received positive reviews from audiences and was a box office success, grossing $278 million worldwide on a $19 million budget.

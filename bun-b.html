Bun B

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Sunday, May 19, 2024

Bernard Freeman, better known as Bun B, is an American rapper. He is well known as one half of the southern rap duo UGK (Underground Kingz), which he co-founded with Pimp C in 1987. Bun B has released four solo albums in addition to his work with UGK, including Trill OG in 2010, which received a rare 5 mic rating from The Source.

When did Bun B decide to pursue a career in music?
In the late 1980s, Pimp C formed the rap team UGK. He first released a demo tape called “Underground King” that didn’t include Bun B. Alternatively, it included Mitchell Queen, a buddy of Pimp C’s. Bun and his friend Jalon Jackson formed P.A. Militia, which was their own band. The two organizations merged to form the 4 Black Ministers group.
Later, Queen and Jackson quit the group, leaving Pimp C and Bun B, who decided to rejoin UGK. Underground King, a demo Pimp C recorded with Mitchell Queen, caught the attention of Bigtyme Records, a Houston-based independent label. They then published The Southern Way, an extended play.
Jive Records took notice of this album when it was released. Many of the songs on their debut album, Too Hard to Swallow, had content that was too explicit for the album, therefore Bigtyme Records published an extended play named Banned, which included all of the songs that were too explicit for Too Hard to Swallow, before the album was released. The song “Pocket Full of Stones” was the first by UGK to receive widespread exposure in the United States. In 1994, they released their second album, Super Tight… Ridin’ Dirty, their third album, reached number two on the R&B charts and number 15 on the Billboard 200 in 1996. Pimp C was sentenced to prison after releasing their fourth studio album, Dirty Money.
In January 2014, Bun B announced “The Trillest Tour.” On January 30, the Houston native announced his tour dates, which he would be joined by Kirko Bangz, another Houston native. From March 6 through April 17, 2014, the tour was on the road.
Aside from music, he and author Shea Serrano collaborated on a coloring book named “Bun B’s Rap Coloring Book” in 2013. Freeman and Serrano have previously discussed collaborating on a book. Initially, they intended to collaborate on a “hip hop survival guide,” but they later decided against it. Serrano later drew and released coloring pages of rappers on Tumblr. The pages rapidly became popular. After that, Freeman and Serrano decided to create an entire coloring book.
He is also a Distinguished Lecturer on the Rice University faculty. He offered a Hip-Hop and Religion course at the School of the Humanities.

Is Bub B really dead?

On Bun B’s Facebook page, rumors of his death drew nearly a million “likes.” The following is a credible narrative of the American rapper’s death for those who read this page:

This is what was written on the board;

“Our favorite rapper Bun B passed away on Monday (December 03, 2018) about 11 a.m. ET. Bun B was born in Port Arthur on March 19, 1973. He will be sorely missed, but he will not be forgotten. Please express your sorrow and sympathies by leaving a comment and liking this page.”

Many of his followers instantly began posting condolence notes on his Facebook page, expressing their sorrow that the talented 45-year-old rapper had passed away. And, as expected, the death hoax sent the Twittersphere into a frenzy.

Some admirers believed the post, while others did not. Some speculated that the news had not been broadcast on any major American network, implying that it was a hoax, as the death of a rapper of Bun B’s caliber would be big news on all networks.

Bun B’s representatives formally acknowledged his death on Tuesday, December 4th. “He joins a lengthy list of celebrities who have fallen prey to the hoax. Stop believe anything you see on the Internet, he’s still alive and well,” they stated.

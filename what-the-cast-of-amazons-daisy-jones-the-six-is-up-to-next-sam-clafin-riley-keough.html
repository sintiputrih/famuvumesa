What The Cast Of Amazon's Daisy Jones &amp; The Six Is Up To Next

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Sunday, August 18, 2024

While it's not the first book to screen adaptation to become a phenomenon by any means, Amazon's miniseries Daisy Jones & The Six has quickly become one of the most beloved and talked about since its premiere in March 2023.

The show, based on the book of the same name by author Taylor Jenkins Reid (inspired by Fleetwood Mac), follows a rock band's rise and fall, particularly from the perspective of its two singers - Billy Dunne and Daisy Jones - through interviews in a documentary after the band's breakup years later.

With just ten episodes, the show managed to capture not just fans of the novel but those who had never turned a page, causing viewers to fall in love with the fictional band and with the real music performed by the cast - which has been expanded upon after rumors have popped up about the cast actually going on tour in the near future.

But what does the cast of Daisy Jones & The Six have in store next? What projects are we most excited to see each of them tackle?

9 Riley Keough

As part of rock 'n' roll royalty as the grandchild of Elvis Presley, it's no surprise that Riley Keough has shined as both a performer and a singer - first showing her chops in the 2010 film, The Runaways.

Outside of starring as Daisy Jones, Keough also had a recent role in the Amazon series The Terminal List in 2022, which has been renewed for a second season. Besides future seasons of The Terminal List, Keough will also star as main character, Rebecca Godfrey, in the upcoming Hulu true crime drama, Under the Bridge.

Related: How Elvis' Granddaughter, Riley Keough, Earns Her Money

8 Sam Claflin

Perhaps the most recognizable member of the cast, Sam Claflin is also no stranger to book to screen adaptations. He's starred as Finnick Odair in The Hunger Games franchise, Will Traynor in the film Me Before You, and Richard Sharp in the survival drama, Adrift, just to name a few.

Besides rumors of his involvement in Daisy Jones & The Six going on tour, there hasn't been much buzz about his future projects. He's set to star in a horror film called The Bagman and lend his voice to an animated kids film that explores the galaxy in Watch the Skies.

7 Camila Morrone

A relative newcomer from the world of modeling, Camila Morrone has been gaining traction since 2018 with her role in the stoner comedy, Never Goin' Back, after first acting in James Franco's film, Bukowski, back in 2013.

Her upcoming projects include Gonzo Girl, based on the novel of the same name by Cheryl Della Pietra - which will be Patricia Arquette's debut as a feature director. She will also be starring in the romantic thriller, Marmalade, as the titular character, opposite Stranger Things actor Joe Keery.

6 Tom Wright

A legend of both the screen and stage, Wright has starred in hundreds of projects across all mediums, with his most recent in Daisy Jones & The Six as the beloved producer of the band who puts Daisy Jones and The Six together.

At the moment, only one future project has been announced and is in pre-production - the comedy feature, Summer Camp, which finds three childhood friends reunite for at a summer camp, which they used to attend together every summer when they were kids.

5 Suki Waterhouse

Another cast member who started in modeling, Suki Waterhouse has quickly made a name for herself as an actress since her first film, Pusher, back in 2012. She's even starred alongside fellow cast member Sam Claflin, in the 2014 romantic comedy film, Love, Rosie.

While very little is known about her future projects, her most recent role in film was in 2022's Dalíland, which explored the marriage of Salvador Dalí and Gala.

Outside of acting, Waterhouse launched an accessories brand in 2016 with her best friend, Poppy Jamie, titled Pop & Suki - which have been worn by a catalog of celebrities including Lady Gaga and Jessica Alba.

4 Josh Whitehouse

Josh Whitehouse is another cast member of the band that's a relative newcomer, with a few minor roles in film and television. His biggest roles have been on Poldark, starring in numerous episodes as the character Hugh Armitage, and as Sir Cole in the Christmas rom-com, The Knight Before Christmas, starring opposite Vanessa Hudgens.He was set to play a leading role in a Game of Thrones prequel series, but it was scrapped.

Whitehouse's future projects include lending his voice to an animated film set in the rain forest, Ozi - Voice of the Forest, a horror film called The Last Moon, and a drama in post-production called The Happy Worker.

3 Will Harrison

It seems like many of the band members in The Six were given their breakthrough roles on the show, including Will Harrison. Before joining the show as Graham Dunne, Harrison starred in a handful of short films and an episode of Madam Secretary.

But after finding massive success on Daisy Jones & The Six, it wouldn't be surprising if he starts popping up in frequently in the future.

He's already set to star in an upcoming series that explores the search for the assassin of President Lincoln, titled Manhunt, which has a planned premiere for August 2023. He's also spent some time in the theatre realm, as part of the play The Coast Starlight in 2023.

2 Sebastian Chacon

As the carefree drummer, Warren Rojas, Sebastian Chacon has reached a newfound popularity with Daisy Jones & The Six, but before joining the show he had a career in theatre and guest starred on numerous tv shows, including Mr. Robot, Narcos, and Pose. His first major role came as Fly Rico in the Penny Dreadful: City of Angels series.

Not much is known about his upcoming projects, besides a thriller titled Humane, which explores a world where humanity must get rid of twenty percent of the population and a family torn apart because of the system.

1 Nabiyah Be

Another cast member that got their start in the world of theatre (originating Eurydice in the musical Hadestown Off-Broadway), Nabiyah Be's breakthrough role came with Daisy Jones & The Six as the disco singer Simone Jackson.

Before that, she appeared in the short film White Wedding and had a minor role in Marvel's Black Panther.

Little is known about her future, but after proving both her acting and singing abilities it looks rather bright.
